Load Glitch is a monster—or rather now a glitch—that was never meant to survive. He was a mistake of the creator, one that they wished to forget and erase. But instead, it gave birth to a creature that would never forgive anyone. He will strive to take absolutely everything that exists into his own hands. And it doesn’t matter whether it was yours or not—he will take it and make it his.

Backstory

This was an ordinary Sans from yet another alternative universe, which in its concept and idea did not differ much from Underswap, Storyshift, and similar ones. In this universe, there was absolutely nothing new or distinctive compared to others, but even so, its inhabitants were quite happy and did not worry about their uniqueness compared to other universes, living just like everyone else.
But the author did not think so. He considered his universe nothing more than a ridiculous idea that he had failed to properly develop or refine in a way that would make it stand out among others. He realized it was time to create something truly new and worthy—and to bury and forget the old.
And so, one day in the Underground, all familiar life was destroyed. The entire world was tearing apart at the seams, and its inhabitants were disappearing—not even turning into dust, but into fragments of code. Sans saw all of this and was utterly horrified. He tried to help, but it was useless. When he finally found his brother, Papyrus pushed him away, trying to save his life. Papyrus was vanishing right before Sans’s eyes, becoming nothing, while Sans, by sheer luck, fell through his own world and escaped that terrible fate. He himself was transported into a completely чужой world he knew nothing about, while his body writhed in agony from the pain he felt—as if he were breaking apart from the inside and unable to stop it.
By some miracle—or perhaps misfortune—Sans survived. And not just survived. He was in Snowdin! It felt as if everything that had happened was just a dream, but the tearing pain inside reminded him that he had not been asleep, and everything was real. His body began to be overtaken by strange glitches that behaved like parasites. Sans needed help, and not far from his usual post, he noticed someone. Carefully approaching, he called out—and saw the impossible. Before him stood himself—another Sans. In different clothes, more tired, but undeniably him. This Sans was not friendly; in fact, he seemed frightened and reacted with aggression. The suffering Sans tried to explain, to ask for help, but the other would not listen, and Sans had no choice but to flee to the Ruins, to Toriel.
Reaching the Ruins, Sans realized that the path was blocked. He pounded on the doors with all his strength, shouting for help and calling out to Toriel. Sans still did not understand where he was or what was happening, but he knew one thing—this was not his home. He kept knocking and knocking, without stopping, but nobody came. The pain in his body continued to grow relentlessly, making existence itself unbearable.
He called for Toriel, called for anyone at all, but nobody came. When he had almost no strength left, he sat down, leaning against the door, still begging for someone—anyone—to help him.
But nobody came.
Overwhelmed by the pain tearing through his entire body, Sans began to lose consciousness. And then he heard a voice. Disgusting, barely intelligible, and heavy. It somewhat resembled his own voice, but heavily distorted—like an audio recording stuck and glitching. The voice said it was ready to help him. Ready to take away his pain. Ready to free him from suffering. Sans doubted the intentions of the voice, and upon hearing Papyrus’s voice in the distance, he came to his senses, believing his brother had returned for him. Opening his eyes, he carefully stood up—however, instead of his brother, he saw someone resembling him. And beside him stood that other Sans, along with the Royal Guard. Sans tensed as they surrounded him, but he still held onto hope of resolving the misunderstanding. He slowly approached this Papyrus, thinking that, like his own brother, he would listen and help him. But he was wrong.
In that very moment, this Papyrus shattered his skull with his weapon, breaking the right side of his face along with his eye. Horror, shock, pain—all merged into one within him. His hope for salvation faltered, and upon hearing mockery from this skeleton, it was completely destroyed. In this world, there was no salvation, no hope, no chance of receiving help. And then the voice returned. It said it knew this would happen and openly laughed at Sans. Now Sans accepted its help—anything to end the pain and suffering. However, the voice said something Sans could never have imagined:
— Since when do I need your permission?
While the local Papyrus and Sans in Snowdin were deciding what to do with the body of this unknown figure, he suddenly stood up, and a sinister, maniacal, spine-chilling smile slowly appeared on his face. Without hesitation, he attacked the local Sans and, grabbing him by the neck, instantly tore off his head as if it were nothing. Papyrus tried to retaliate immediately, but Sans instantly teleported to him and pierced straight through his chest, grabbing his soul. Papyrus looked in horror and confusion at the face of the one he had just killed, while the new Sans only smirked and thanked him for everything. In the next moment, he destroyed his soul, and Papyrus’s body fell onto the snow—not turning into dust, which was even stranger. From that very moment, the universe was doomed.
A day passed, and the entire universe was emptied. Now he was alone in this world. When there was no one left to destroy, sanity awakened within him, and he realized that this world was a parallel reality—similar to his former home, yet different. He became curious whether other worlds like this existed—perhaps even stranger ones. But he could not find the answer. He was stuck here, and all he could do was wait for something.
And he did. Another Sans, almost identical to him, suddenly appeared in his world. However, this one was completely black and had tentacles. He was impressed by the scale of destruction. Of course, he also fell victim to his attack—but killing him was not possible. Nightmare grabbed him by the neck and said that he could give him the opportunity to leave this place and visit other worlds—if he served him. Not the best offer, but realizing he had no advantage, he agreed to become (temporarily) his servant.
In the end, he discovered many new things: meeting other Sanses, visiting other AUs, learning about the multiverse and its creators. All of this formed a clear understanding of what caused the destruction of his world—and what his goal now was. But to achieve it, he needed to become independent. And as long as he relied on Nightmare for teleportation between AUs, that was impossible. Fortunately, he knew who could help him.
One day, when they crossed paths with Error, he deceived Nightmare’s group of skeletons and attacked Error. Neither Error nor Nightmare expected this. He immobilized Error and began trying to extract all possible information from his mind, while Nightmare ordered him to stop. Realizing it was useless, a tentacle threw him away from Error, allowing the latter to recover and react in anger. But now it no longer mattered. He had obtained what he wanted—and burst into the most insane laughter he had ever produced. Now nothing restrained him. He was free and could do whatever he wished. Without wasting a second, he teleported in an unknown direction. After this, Nightmare could no longer contain his rage and fury. A new, great threat had emerged in the multiverse. A threat that sought to enslave the entire multiverse and become its master. And its name became Load Glitch, the Enslaver of Universes.
